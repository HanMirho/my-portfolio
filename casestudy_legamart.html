Some context

LegaMart is a UK-based platform that aims connect clients who have legal matters to take care of abroad to lawyers in the target country.

1.The problem

At Legamart, we were facing a ~90% bounce rate in the free consult request user journey.

(sorry about the image quality. It's old.)

2.Discovery

With heuristics and a touch of common sense, we came to the conclusion that the problem was, quite obviously, the confusing and long application journey.

3.The process

We set a few goals for our redesign of the flow. We determined that the form should:

  • Be Simple
    It shouldn't be a maze to navigate, and kept to 2-3 steps.
  • Be Easy to understand & accurate
    It should acquire the required legal information as accurately as possible, without getting the user stuck in a quagmire of legal terms.
  • Be Reassuring & empathetic
    People facing legal trouble are stressed and frustrated. We should be reassuring, supportive, and kind.
  • Increase signup & reduce bounce rates.
    And for that, we create a **sunk cost fallacy**. We just ask the user about their problem before getting their info and asking them to sign up. Nothing evil.
So, in short, it should be legible, likable, and legal.
This is where we started doing brainstorming and wireframing for the form's IA, and testing microcopies.
